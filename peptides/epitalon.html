What is Epitalon?

Epitalon is a synthetic tetrapeptide with the sequence Ala-Glu-Asp-Gly. It was originally derived from extracts of bovine pineal glands, where it plays roles in regulating circadian rhythms and pineal function. Modern Epitalon is synthetically produced.

How Epitalon Works

Epitalon's primary mechanisms include supporting pineal gland function and promoting telomerase activation. Telomerase is an enzyme that extends telomeres (protective caps on chromosomes), which may support cellular longevity. Additionally, by supporting the pineal gland, Epitalon restores natural circadian rhythm regulation and melatonin production.

Telomerase and Longevity Benefits

Research suggests Epitalon activates telomerase, potentially extending cellular lifespan markers. Longer telomeres are associated with better health outcomes and slower biological aging. This makes Epitalon of particular interest to longevity-focused biohackers.

Dosing

Safety

Excellent safety profile with no serious adverse events reported. Well-tolerated chronically. Minimal side effects. Non-toxic at therapeutic doses.

Research: Studies by Khavinson et al. demonstrate Epitalon's telomerase-activating effects and circadian rhythm restoration capabilities in aging models.
